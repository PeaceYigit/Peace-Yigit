## ğŸ“„ Core Components of a Good Security Report

### 1. Executive Summary
- High-level overview
- Business impact
- Severity explanation
- No technical jargon

This is often the most-read section.

---

### 2. Vulnerability Description
- What the issue is
- Where it exists
- Why it is a problem

Clarity is more important than complexity.

---

### 3. Impact Assessment
- What could realistically happen
- Who or what is affected
- Worst-case vs likely scenario

Impact defines priority.

---

### 4. Proof of Concept (Minimal & Safe)
- Demonstrates the issue
- Avoids unnecessary damage
- Respects scope and ethics

Proof, not exploitation.

---

### 5. Remediation Recommendations
- Clear and actionable
- Prefer prevention over detection
- Aligned with best practices

Good recommendations increase trust.
